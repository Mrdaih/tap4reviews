On paper, QR codes are the obvious winner: free, printable on any receipt, works on every phone. NFC requires a physical card, a chip, and a little bit of setup. And yet, when we test the two side-by-side in actual restaurants, **NFC wins by a wide margin** — typically 2–3× the review conversion rate.

Here's why.

## The scan friction nobody talks about

A QR code looks easy. It isn't. To scan one, the customer has to:

1. Pick up their phone and unlock it.
2. Swipe to find the camera app (or, worse, open a QR scanner app).
3. Align the phone at the right distance and angle.
4. Wait for the link to appear.
5. Tap the link.

In a dim restaurant, at the end of a meal, with a bill in one hand — those five steps are enough to kill the impulse. Half the time the camera won't focus, or the link banner is buried, and the customer quietly gives up.

## NFC collapses the flow

With NFC, the flow is:

1. Tap phone on card.
2. Tap "Open" on the banner that appears.

That's it. Two taps. No aiming. No apps. No waiting. The phone doesn't even need to be fully awake — the screen lights up automatically when NFC is detected.

In our field tests, the median time from "card visible" to "review page open" is 2.8 seconds on NFC vs. 14 seconds on QR. That 11-second gap is where most conversions die.

## When QR still wins

We don't tell people to pick one or the other — we ship every Tap4Reviews card with both. QR codes still matter for:

- **iPads and tablets** used as menus.
- **The handful of phones** that genuinely lack NFC.
- **Group settings** where passing the card is impractical — someone can snap the QR from across the table.

The right question isn't NFC vs QR. It's: *how do we remove every second of friction between a happy guest and a 5-star review?* The answer, almost always, is both together, with NFC as the primary call-to-action.

## Cost per review

Run the math. A single NFC card pays for itself the moment it drives 5–10 additional reviews — which, on a busy restaurant floor, takes less than a week. Every review after that is effectively free.

QR codes cost nothing, but if they're converting at half the rate, they cost you the reviews you're not getting. In restaurant economics, the gap between 2 reviews a day and 5 reviews a day is a competitor growing 2.5× faster than you.
